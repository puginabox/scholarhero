Company

Scholar Hero is a software company that designs web and mobile applications for safe, constructive, and engaged collaboration between students and scholars.

Mission

Scholar Hero wants to remove the frustrations that exist in the academic career advancement by providing tools and features that restore confidence, share from anywhere, and workshop ideas in a constructive forum.

Description

Scholar Hero has unique features to help scholars build confidence, get connected, and display their works-in-progress securely. Scholar Hero is also a place for scholars to workshop their work and gain a competitive edge in publishing, fundraising, or academic coursework.

Conceived: SW:PGH

Scholar Hero began as a conversation at Startup Weekend: Pittsburgh, about making a real difference in university education. Months later, Scholar Hero, Inc. officially formed and commenced its mission.

Incubated: Hustle Den

Selected as one of the finalists of the highly-competitive Business Bout, Scholar Hero was invited to be a member of the inaugural class of The Hustle Den, Pittsburgh's newest incubator. Hustle Den is owned by The Thrill Mill, a 501(c)(3)-pending not-for-profit organization.

Stationed: Pittsburgh

Considered widely as one of the top places in the nation to launch a company, Pittsburgh provides Scholar Hero with an immense, cutting-edge technical capital while keeping operating costs affordable. It's the perfect Lean Startup environment.

Team

The League of Scholar Heroes

Lee Ngo Founder, Executive Director

Over his professional career, Lee has worn many different hats: graphic designer, filmmaker, television producer, university instructor, anthropologist, and now education advocate.

Apart from his scholarly pursuits, Lee previously worked for Tribune Broadcasting and worked with various non-profit organizations in multimedia and education. He hopes to make a difference in the culture of the academy to ensure that the brightest of society don't get left behind.

He is a graduate of Yale University and the University of California, Irvine.

Michael Butler Technical Director

Michael is a software engineer, both self-taught and professionally educated in a dozen programming and scripting languages. As the primary technical architect of Scholar Hero, Michael hopes to create an enduring legacy that raises the bar on education and scholarship online.

Mike attended the University of Pittsburgh and is an honors graduate of the Pittsburgh Technical Institute.
